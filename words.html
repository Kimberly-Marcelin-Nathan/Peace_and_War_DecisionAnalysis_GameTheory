THE PEACE-WAR GAME
You have one choice. In front of you is a machine: if you put a coin in the machine, the other player gets three coins – and vice versa. You both can either choose PEACE (put in coin), or WAR (don't put in coin).

Let's say the other player chooses war, and doesn't put in a coin.
What should you do?

Exactly! Why let that moocher mooch off of you?

Alas, turning the other cheek just gets you slapped!

If you choose peace & they choose war, you lose a coin while they gain +3. (score: -1 vs +3) However, if you both choose war, neither of you gain or lose anything. (score: 0 vs 0) Therefore: choose WAR XD.

But let's say the other player chooses peace, and puts in a coin.
What should you do now?

Wow, that's mean... and also the correct answer!

Sure, seems like the right thing to do... OR IS IT??

Because if you both choose peace, you both give up a coin to gain +3. (score: +2 vs +2) But if you choose war & they choose peace, you gain +3 coins at their cost of one. (score: +3 vs -1) Therefore: "should" choose WAR XD.

And that's our dilemma. Trust is nice, but it can let others take advantage of you -- or shoot you as you come unarmed out of a trench. Sometimes, distrust is rational! But now, what happens if we play this game...
